Covid-19

Everything you need to know about our COVID-19 plan

* Please note that this document is suggested as a guide and is subject to change at any time due to the ongoing and unforeseen changes associated with the COVID-19 pandemic.

WEEKLY

We will send an email weekly to families confirming the following:

  • Has your child or anyone in your family started to exhibit symptoms of COVID 19 in the last 48 hours including fever, dry cough or shortness of breath?
  • Has your child or anyone in your family been around anyone in the last 48 hours who has started to exhibit symptoms of COVID 19 in the last 14 days including fever, dry cough or shortness of breath?
  • Has your child or anyone in your family left the province/country in the last 14 days?

DAILY

Before you arrive at camp

  • Please ensure the screening email is completed weekly.
  • Please ensure your child is dressed appropriately in all biking gear including pads, helmet, gloves and camel back with a snack and water in their camel back. Please ensure your child is prepared and dressed for any weather, to avoid the need to go inside.
  • Please ensure if your child’s coach has requested something on the bike be altered that this has been done (brakes, seat height, pedals on or off etc). We will have limited time to alter bikes in the morning with our COVID procedures.

When your child arrives at camp

  • You will be asked to enter through the front door and exit through the rear of the building. The sign in areas will be separated into groups and your coach will meet you and your child. Siblings will be able to arrive at the same time. You may be asked to wait to enter the building if there are too many people in the building, please be patient we will get to you as soon as we can!
  • Upon arrival your child's temperature will be taken with an infrared thermometer and if your child's temperature is above 38 degrees celsius we will ask you to wait to the side for 5 minutes and we will take the temperature again. If your child's temperature remains above 38 degrees after this we will ask you to go home.
  • Your child's coach will ask you the COVID pre screening questions every morning and we ask that you please answer the questions honestly.
  • Your child's group will depart within the time frame allocated. If you miss your drop off time, you will need to organise with your child’s coach to meet at an allocated secondary location once the group arrives there.

During the day of camp

  • In order to protect children and staff, we are utilizing multiple measures to maximize health and safety at camp during a typical day:
  • If an instructor is working with a beginner biker and close contact/support is required, PPE has been identified by health officials as suitable mitigation for this situation. This is a similar approach being used by barbers, hairstylists and any other personal service that requires close proximity between worker and customer. Also note that instructors are advised to limit physical contact with children and support the bike as opposed to the child wherever possible. Instructors and children should always wear their bike gloves.
  • Our staff all have first aid training and will use their best judgement to balance the need for wound treatment and emotional support, with the need to limit close contact. If needed we will call 911 or refer to the non emergency line.
  • Campers stay together in their smaller classes for the day with the same instructor where possible and a tail gunner where possible to help promote social distancing and we will stay outside when possible to give children room to keep their distance from each other. Small group sizes, ideally with children who already know or play with each other, can help reduce the likelihood of virus transmission if children are placed into groups within their own social networks.
  • Classes will be distanced between each other and will mostly be riding trails based south of the village as we are more easily able to promote social distancing on the trails.
  • If it becomes too difficult to manage physical distancing measures with any children, coaches will immediately stop the activity and focus on keeping the children safe and at least 6ft (2m) apart. We will take regular breaks; tired children tend to forget rules and stop listening!
  • We will also practice physical distancing measures through games and drills, making it part of the fun to learn about how and why children need to respect each other’s space, during COVID-19.
  • Staggered and separate snack and lunch locations and times.
  • No non-essential visitors allowed. If you need to drop off late or pick up early this will be at the discretion of the coach or manager on duty that day.

End of Day

  • We will ask you to wait outside until we call you in to collect your child. While you wait please practice social distancing!

We will have a rigorous sanitizing program including:

  • Each group will have their own basket for their lunches, swim clothes, balls etc and the basket and balls will be sanitized at the end of each day by the coach. The basket will also have hand sanitizer, soap, baby wipes and sanitizer to wipe down handles etc.
